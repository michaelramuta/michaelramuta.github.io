Problems in Technology: Women and Programming

May 1, 2015

It's no secret that women are severely under represented in many of the STEM fields, and the fields obviously suffer for it. According to NPR (Source) only 20% of programmers are female.

This is a problem, not only because it excludes women from a growing (and high paying) section of the workforce, but also because teams will strongly benefit from having more diverse members.

There are a few potential strategies for increasing the role of women in programming:

  1. Provide scholarships to women interested in programming.
  2. Promote societies and groups designed to provide support for women in programming.
  3. Get young women interested in programming from an early age, by reaching out to middle and high school students.
  4. Educate businesses on the benefits of increasing diversity and providing opportunities for women.
